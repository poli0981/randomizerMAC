# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and productive environment for everyone. This project values directness, efficiency, and mutual respect.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be direct.** Get to the point. No need for lengthy greetings, formalities, or small talk. Open your issue or PR and state what you need.
- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions.
- Focus on the technical merits of contributions and feedback.
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully.
- Use clear, concise language. Avoid being vague or overly generic.
- Respect the time and effort of maintainers and contributors.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Insulting, derogatory, or harassing language directed at any individual.
- Personal attacks, trolling, or deliberately inflammatory remarks.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Spam, off-topic content, or content unrelated to this project.
- Submitting PRs containing malicious code or suspected malware.
- Excessively vague, rambling, or misleading reports that waste maintainers' time.

### A Note on Respect

You are free to dislike this project, criticize its design, or choose not to use it. However, insulting or demeaning the maintainers or other contributors without cause reflects on your own character, not theirs. We will not tolerate it.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication channels associated with this repository.

## Enforcement

Violations will be handled on a case-by-case basis:

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Minor (first offense) | Warning via comment |
| Repeated or moderate | Temporary ban (7-30 days) |
| Severe (threats, malware, doxxing) | **Permanent ban** |

### Auto-Ignore Policy

The following will be **ignored without response**:

- Submissions containing offensive, hateful, or abusive language toward maintainers.
- PRs containing malicious code or suspected backdoors.
- Issues or PRs completely unrelated to this project.
- Excessively long, vague, or unfocused reports that do not clearly describe the issue.

## Reporting

To report a violation, open an issue with the label `conduct` or contact the maintainer through GitHub.
